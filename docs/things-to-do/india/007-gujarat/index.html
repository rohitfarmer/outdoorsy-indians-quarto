Outdoorsy Things to Do in Gujarat, India

India
A list of outdoor activities to do in Gujarat, India.
Published

September 12, 2023

Keywords

Outdoors, Things to do, Gujarat, India

Gujarat, located on the western coast of India, is known for its rich history, architectural wonders, and vibrant culture. However, it also offers a plethora of outdoor activities and natural wonders that are often overlooked. From pristine beaches to wildlife sanctuaries, and from desert landscapes to serene lakes, Gujarat has it all. Here’s a guide to outdoorsy things you can indulge in when visiting this magnificent state:

  1. The White Rann of Kutch (cover image of this post) : The Rann of Kutch is a salt marsh in the Thar Desert. Its ethereal beauty peaks during the full moon nights. The Rann Utsav, a cultural festival, is a great time to visit and experience the local music, dance, and crafts, amidst this lunar-like landscape.

  2. Gir National Park : The last bastion of the Asiatic lion, this park is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts. Apart from the lions, one can also spot leopards, antelopes, and a variety of bird species. Opt for a safari tour for a thrilling adventure.

  3. Marine National Park, Narara Island : Situated near Jamnagar, it’s India’s first marine sanctuary. With a low tide, one can walk around the park and spot octopuses, pufferfish, crabs, and even dolphins in the distant waters.

  4. Trekking in Saputara : Saputara is Gujarat’s only hill station. It offers various trekking and hiking trails amidst the Sahyadri range. The Governor’s Hill is a popular trek, offering panoramic views of the entire hill station and the adjoining valleys.

  5. Beaches of Gujarat : Mandvi, Dwarka, and Diu are among the most popular beaches. The Mandvi beach is known for its serenity and camel rides, while Dwarka, apart from its religious significance, offers mesmerizing sunset views.

  6. Paragliding in Saputara : For those seeking an adrenaline rush, paragliding over the Sahyadri range offers an exhilarating experience. Get a bird’s eye view of the lush greenery, lakes, and undulating landscapes.

  7. Camping at Blackbuck National Park : Located in Velavadar, this park is home to a large population of the Indian Blackbuck. Camping under the stars and waking up to the sight of these elegant creatures is an experience to cherish.

  8. Boating in Nal Sarovar Bird Sanctuary : A paradise for bird watchers, Nal Sarovar houses more than 200 species of birds. Rent a boat at dawn to witness the spectacular view of migratory birds taking flight.

  9. Dinosaur Fossil Park at Raiyoli : Believe it or not, Gujarat also has a connection with the prehistoric era. Raiyoli near Balasinor is known to be the third-largest dinosaur fossil site in the world and also houses a fossilized dinosaur egg hatchery.

  10. Wild Ass Sanctuary : Situated in the Little Rann of Kutch, this sanctuary is home to the endangered Indian Wild Ass. Other inhabitants include flamingoes, pelicans, and cranes. Opt for a jeep safari for the best experience.

  11. Thol Lake Bird Sanctuary : Just a short drive from Ahmedabad, Thol Lake is another bird watcher’s paradise, especially during the winter months when it welcomes various migratory species.

  12. Cycling Tours : Several agencies offer cycling tours across Gujarat. Popular routes cover coastal areas, heritage sites, and the countryside, allowing tourists to experience Gujarat from a unique perspective.

  13. Khijadiya Bird Sanctuary : Located near Jamnagar, this sanctuary boasts of a mix of freshwater lakes, salt pans, and marshy lands, providing diverse habitats for different bird species.

Conclusion

Visiting Gujarat provides an opportunity to delve deep into its natural beauty, making it an enriching experience for every kind of traveler. Whether you’re an adventure seeker, wildlife enthusiast, or just someone looking for serenity amidst nature, Gujarat promises an unforgettable outdoor escapade.
